Biography



My name is Simon and I’m a student at Baruch College. I’m also a programmer!

I was born in New York City but at age six when my parents split, I found a new home in Melbourne, Australia where I would spend the next thirteen years of my life. Now, I’ve returned to further my education and establish my career. Throughout my life, I've travelled between six countries around the world, and I truly believe that there is no place better for me to develop the skills and network to achieve my ambitions. If you're here to learn more about me, I invite you to explore my website.

Career Interests


Software Development

I believe that in the next few years, global disruptive changes will continue to occur in the field of information technology and computer science. In order to take advantage of this change, I strive to understand it so that I can lead rather than follow.

Entrepreneurship

I believe that my personality naturally leads me toward the path of entrepreneurship. In life, the output of success should reflect the input of thought and hard work. Being an entrepreneur would give me the opportunity to take control of my life and reach my maximum potential to succeed.

Financial Investment

With time and labor as limited resources, I realized the importance of having my assets work for me. Aiming to expand my knowledge in this field and to refine my own investment philosophy, I've read numerous Investopedia articles and many of Berkshire Hathaway's letters to shareholders written by Warren Buffet.

Personal Interests


Health and Fitness

I used to be a bit of a fitness freak, so I know quite a lot about diet and proper form for workouts and scheduling. Unfortunately, there's some difference between knowing what to do and actually doing it…

Animals

Anyone who knows me knows that I'm a huge fan of animals! I’ve owned two pets so far (a dog and a cat), but I’m also interested in the habits of non-domesticated animals. To me, animals symbolize a simpler way of life.

Travelling

Throughout my life, I’ve travelled between America, Australia, China, Hong Kong, Singapore and spent one day in Korea (I got the stamp on my passport so it counts). I think that travelling helps us break free from complacency by broadening our perspective through exposure to new cultures and ideologies.


Video Games

I’ve played video games probably since before I could read; although I haven't had the time to do so recently. I'm a big fan of pretty much anything Blizzard, especially StarCraft II. I’ve also played DotA since second grade.

TV Shows

Who doesn’t like to wind down and watch a good show after a long day? Although I don’t have time to watch too many shows these days, my favorite ones so far are The Office, Breaking Bad and Friends. I’m also a fan of some anime, Korean and Chinese films.

Chess

Physics and Astronomy

Nothing makes me feel humbler than to ponder that I'm but one individual on one of many planets in one of many solar systems in one of many galaxies in the universe. I've always been curious about the things beyond our view, which is how I developed an interest in astronomy and by extension – physics.

Psychology

People are all unique, but deep down most of us follow the same set of instincts, some of which are primal, others which have been shaped by social conditioning. I love the challenge of trying to learn things that are hard to understand, and people always find a way to surprise.

Philosophy

I think that the capacity to ponder some of the world’s most fundamental questions is what separates us from the animals that live their lives instinctively. Some of my favorite philosophers include Rene Descartes and his work on epistemology as well as Friedrich Nietzsche and his work on ethics.

Personal Projects


Square Hero

A desktop application similar to Flappy Bird, written in Python using the Pygame library. Implemented algorithms to handle features such as scrolling, resizing and collision detection.

TicBot

An unbeatable Tic-Tac-Toe artificial intelligence. Written in Python and executed through the desktop Command Prompt window.

Breakout

A desktop application resembling the classic arcade game Breakout, written in C using the Stanford Portable Library as a project for Harvard’s online Computer Science course, CS50.


StackIT
